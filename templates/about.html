Model Feature Definitions

Understanding the 9 clinical inputs used for prediction.

The patient's age in years.

The patient's biological sex.

  • 1: Male
  • 0: Female

A classification of the patient's chest pain.

  • 0 (Typical Angina): Chest pain related to reduced blood flow to the heart, typically induced by exertion.
  • 1 (Atypical Angina): Chest pain that is less typical of angina.
  • 2 (Non-anginal Pain): Chest pain not believed to be heart-related.
  • 3 (Asymptomatic): No chest pain reported.

The highest heart rate (in beats per minute) measured during an exercise stress test.

Whether the patient experienced chest pain during the exercise test.

  • 1: Yes
  • 0: No

ST depression induced by exercise relative to rest. This is a key measurement from an EKG that indicates heart stress.

The slope of the peak exercise ST segment on the EKG.

  • 0 (Upsloping): ST segment is sloping upwards.
  • 1 (Flat): ST segment is horizontal (flat).
  • 2 (Downsloping): ST segment is sloping downwards.

The number of major coronary arteries (from 0 to 3) found to be narrowed or blocked, as identified by fluoroscopy (a type of X-ray).

Results from a thallium stress test, which measures blood flow to the heart muscle.

  • 1 (Fixed Defect): A portion of the heart muscle shows no blood flow both at rest and after exercise (likely scar tissue).
  • 2 (Normal): Normal blood flow.
  • 3 (Reversible Defect): Reduced blood flow during exercise that becomes normal at rest (indicates a blockage).
